The Journey from IT Support to Growth Strategist

I'm not your typical marketer. My path started in doctor's offices in Denmark, passed through one of Europe's most famous startup offices, and landed me in Bali — building growth systems for fashion brands worldwide.

How It Started

My first real job was in IT support, fixing computer systems for doctors in my hometown of Herning, Denmark. I had a company car (a Mercedes, actually) and good working conditions. But I knew I wanted something different.

I moved to Copenhagen and landed a role at Jesper Buch's office. If you're Danish, you know who he is — the founder of Just Eat, one of Europe's biggest food delivery successes. Working alongside him and his team for three years taught me what real growth looks like. Not just marketing tricks, but building systems that scale.

The Pivot to Marketing

Four years ago, I moved to Bali. The original plan wasn't marketing — but I started helping friends with their businesses, then their friends, and eventually I had real clients. Blue Sportswear became my first major client, and five years later, we're still working together.

What started as running Pinterest ads evolved into full growth strategy. CRO, retention systems, team management — the whole picture.

My Philosophy

I believe in systems over hacks. Anyone can get lucky with one campaign. I build sustainable growth engines that keep working month after month.

I'm also a firm believer in efficiency. I work 4 hours a day, max. Not because I'm lazy, but because I've learned that focused, high-impact work beats busywork every time.
